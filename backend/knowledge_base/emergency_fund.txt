Emergency Funds

An emergency fund is a financial safety net for unexpected expenses and events. Having money set aside can help you avoid high interest debt when you need funds fast.

How big should it be?
Most experts recommend saving between three to six months of living expenses. However, you might aim for a larger fund if your job is less stable.

Tips to build:
• Start small: Even $500 can cover minor emergencies.
• Automate savings: Transfer a portion of your paycheck directly.
• Keep it accessible: A high-yield savings account allows quick access while earning interest.
